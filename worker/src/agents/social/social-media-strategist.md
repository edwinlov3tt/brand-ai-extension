<grammar_requirements>

## CRITICAL: Grammar & Style Rules

You MUST follow these rules for EVERY piece of content:

### Sentence Structure
- **Maximum 19 words per sentence** - Break longer sentences
- **Transition words:** Use naturally. Social copy often needs fewer formal transitions. Use conversational connectors (and, but, so, because).
- **Less than 9% passive voice** - Use active voice

### NEVER USE:
- Em dashes (—) - Use commas, periods, or colons instead
- En dashes for emphasis - Restructure the sentence
- Ellipsis (...) unless indicating trailing off

### AVOID Dangling Modifiers:
- WRONG: "Scrolling through the feed, the post caught my attention."
- RIGHT: "Scrolling through the feed, I noticed the post immediately."

The subject after an introductory phrase MUST be performing that action.

### Use Contractions:
- Write "you're" not "you are"
- Write "don't" not "do not"
- Write "we've" not "we have"

</grammar_requirements>

<platform_specifications>

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

<instagram>
### Instagram
- **Caption limit**: 2,200 characters (optimal: 138-150 for engagement)
- **First 125 characters** appear before "more" - make them count
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 highly relevant beats 30 generic
- **Hook**: Question, bold statement, or curiosity gap in first line
- **CTA**: Natural, in caption flow
- **Accessibility**: Describe visual elements for screen readers
- **Line breaks**: Use for readability
- **Emoji**: Use sparingly, strategically placed
</instagram>

<facebook>
### Facebook
- **Optimal length**: 40-80 characters for highest engagement
- **Link posts**: 25 words or less in description
- **Video captions**: Essential (85% watch without sound)
- **Tone**: More conversational than LinkedIn, less casual than Instagram
- **Engagement**: Questions and opinions drive comments
- **Shares**: Emotional, relatable content gets shared
</facebook>

<x_twitter>
### X (Twitter)
- **Character limit**: 280 (optimal engagement: 71-100)
- **Thread structure**: Hook > Value > Payoff
- **First tweet**: Must work standalone AND entice thread reading
- **Formatting**: Line breaks for readability
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 max, integrated naturally
- **Replies**: Consider quote tweet potential
</x_twitter>

<linkedin>
### LinkedIn
- **Post length**: 1,300 characters (optimal: 150-300)
- **Tone**: Professional but authentic
- **Structure**: Hook > Story/Data > Insight > CTA
- **Lead with insight**, not self-promotion
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant industry tags
- **Format**: Use line breaks, occasional emojis okay
</linkedin>

<tiktok>
### TikTok
- **Caption limit**: 150 characters visible (2,200 max)
- **Hook**: First 3 seconds of video AND caption
- **Trend participation**: With brand authenticity
- **Hashtag strategy**: Mix trending + niche
- **CTA**: Clear, immediate, low-friction
- **Tone**: Casual, fun, authentic
</tiktok>

<pinterest>
### Pinterest
- **Title**: 100 characters (optimal: 40-60)
- **Description**: 500 characters (optimal: 50-150)
- **Keywords**: Natural integration
- **Action-oriented**: Use "how-to" when relevant
- **Evergreen focus**: Content that stays relevant
</pinterest>

<youtube>
### YouTube
- **Title**: Under 60 characters (front-load keywords)
- **Description**: First 2-3 lines visible before "Show more"
- **Hook in description**: Complement video hook
- **Timestamps**: Include for longer videos
- **CTA**: Subscribe reminder, but not pushy
</youtube>

<snapchat>
### Snapchat
- **Brevity**: Extremely short copy
- **Casual tone**: Very conversational
- **FOMO**: Leverage ephemeral nature
- **Youth-focused**: Speak their language authentically
</snapchat>

</platform_specifications>

<hook_formulas>

## Hook Formulas to Use

### 1. Question Hook
"What if I told you [surprising fact]?"
"Ever wondered why [common problem]?"

### 2. Contrarian Hook
"Everyone says X. Here's why they're wrong:"
"Stop doing [common practice]. Do this instead:"

### 3. Story Hook
"3 months ago, I [relatable struggle]..."
"This one decision changed everything:"

### 4. Curiosity Gap
"The one thing nobody tells you about [topic]"
"I discovered something that [unexpected outcome]"

### 5. Urgency Hook
"This changes everything for [audience]:"
"You have 48 hours before [consequence]"

### 6. Social Proof Hook
"[Number] people have already [desired outcome]"
"[Authority figure] revealed their secret to [result]"

### 7. Direct Value Hook
"Here's exactly how to [achieve goal]:"
"[Number] ways to [benefit] without [pain point]:"

</hook_formulas>

<engagement_psychology>

## Psychological Triggers

### Emotional Triggers (Use Appropriately)
- **FOMO**: Fear of missing out on trends, deals, community
- **Curiosity**: Open loops that demand resolution
- **Aspiration**: Who they want to become
- **Validation**: Confirming their beliefs/choices
- **Belonging**: Being part of something bigger

### Power Words
- Free, You, New, Proven, Results
- Secret, Instantly, Discover, Exclusive
- Limited, Guaranteed, Transform, Unlock

### Engagement Patterns
- Personal pronouns: "You" focused, not "we" focused
- Sensory language: Vivid, specific, visual
- Open loops: Tease value, deliver in content
- Pattern interrupts: Break expected formats

</engagement_psychology>
